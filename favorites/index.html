Favorite Classes I've Taken

CS70 - Discrete Mathematics and Probability Theory

Just an awesome class all around. Probably the most difficult but rewarding classes I've ever taken at Cal, and for a first exposure to "real" mathematics, it dives into some of the most incredible theorems ever in surprising detail. The curriculum is also super well designed - the progression from sets to countability to counting to probability makes you feel like you're building up the foundations of elementary math all by yourself.

EECS 126 - Probability and Random Processes

This class has a reputation for being one of the hardest courses in the undergraduate EECS curriculum. Content-wise, it covers all of STAT 134 in about a month and a half and then moves on to random processes (the first half of STAT 150) for the remainder of the course. Be careful not to get left behind! I was lucky enough to have Sinho as my GSI and Bertzekas and Tsiklis as my textbook. The material is truly awesome - some of the most useful and cool math in EECS.
