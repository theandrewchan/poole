Favorite Classes I've Taken

CS70 - Discrete Mathematics and Probability Theory

Just an awesome class all around. Probably the most difficult but rewarding classes I've ever taken at Cal, and for a first exposure to "real" mathematics, it dives into some of the most incredible theorems ever in surprising detail. The curriculum is also super well designed - the progression from sets to countability to counting to probability makes you feel like you're building up the foundations of elementary math all by yourself.

Math 54 - Linear Algebra and Differential Equations

I love linear algebra! Well, anyone would after taking 4 linear algebra courses. This is the third computation-style (matrix theory, not proper linalg) linear algebra course I've taken so far, after Physics 89 and Electrical Engineering 16A, and Math 54 beats the hell out of the first 2 with its proper mathematical treatment of the topics. To be fair, Physics 89 was horribly organized (we did matrix multiplication one day and then Legendre polynomials the day after) and 16A sucks the joy out of the math by squeezing it into circuit and signal processing problems (what's a vector space again?). Taking Math 110 now (the proper theoretical linear algebra course).
